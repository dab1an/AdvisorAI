## Panther Success Network (PSN)
Source: https://mycs.fiu.edu/psp/stdnt_9/EMPLOYEE/CAMP/s/WEBLIB_HCX_GN.H_DASHBOARD.FieldFormula.IScript_Main?
Type: portal

### Overview
The Panther Success Network (PSN) is FIU’s centralized student support platform used to connect with academic advisors and access important academic tools.

### Key Information
- Students can schedule appointments with academic advisors
- Provides access to:
  - Enrollment dates
  - Messages from advisors or faculty
  - Schedule Builder
  - Degree Planner
- Displays test scores such as:
  - Civics Exam results
  - Placement test scores
  - SAT/ACT scores

## Office of Scholarships
Source: https://scholarships.fiu.edu/
Type: webpage

### Overview
The Office of Scholarships provides information about available scholarships for both incoming and current FIU students, including application guidance and financial resources.

### Key Information
- FIU offers multiple scholarship categories:
  - Merit-based scholarships
  - Departmental scholarships
  - First-generation scholarships
  - External scholarships
  - Prestigious scholarships (e.g., Bright Futures)
- Students can search and apply through the FIU Scholarship Search platform
- Scholarship application periods are most active during the Spring semester (January–March)
- Applying to multiple scholarships increases chances of receiving financial aid

### Financial Wellness Program
- Offers free workshops (virtual and in-person)
- Topics include:
  - Managing student finances
  - Financial literacy
  - Budgeting and financial planning

### Additional Support
- Scholarship advisors are available to guide students through the application process
- Office location: SASC 440, Modesto A. Maidique Campus
- Contact: scholarships@fiu.edu | 305-348-0349

## FIU Financial Aid (OneStop)
Source: https://onestop.fiu.edu/finances/
Type: webpage

### Overview
The FIU Financial Aid (OneStop) portal provides resources and guidance for managing education costs, applying for financial aid, and understanding available funding options.

### Key Information
- Students must apply or reapply for financial aid each academic year
- Financial aid includes:
  - Scholarships
  - Grants (state, institutional, federal)
  - Loans
  - Work-study programs

### Core Services
- Estimate total cost of attendance (tuition, fees, etc.)
- Apply for financial aid (FAFSA and other programs)
- Pay tuition and set up payment plans
- Receive and manage financial aid disbursements
- Access tax documents (e.g., 1098-T)

### Maintaining Financial Aid
- Students must meet:
  - Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) requirements
  - Enrollment requirements
- Failure to meet conditions may result in loss of aid
